# Capital Structure and Float Dynamics

Use this module for equities, recent IPOs/SPACs, capital-intensive companies, small caps, companies funding aggressive growth, or any setup where the business may be real but common-equity upside is threatened by financing terms, dilution, lockups, debt service, or selling overhang.

Core question:

```text
Is financing a bridge to valuable growth, or a machine that transfers upside away from common equity?
```

Do not assume "good company" means "good common stock." A company can execute operationally while ATMs, convertibles, warrants, resale shares, or debt interest consume the equity upside.

## 1. Map the Capital Stack

Build a current capital-stack table:

```text
instrument | amount | maturity/expiry | coupon/cost | conversion/exercise price | security/collateral | covenant | holder/seller | source | risk
```

Include:

- common shares outstanding;
- preferred stock;
- warrants and options;
- convertible notes;
- straight debt, high-interest debt, and credit facilities;
- sale-leasebacks, equipment loans, vendor financing, GPU/asset financing, and project debt;
- strategic prepayments, customer funding, supplier funding, or direct investment;
- PIPEs, resale registrations, lockup shares, earnouts, and contingent consideration.

## 2. Identify Dilution Capacity

Check:

- ATM programs and equity distribution agreements;
- shelf registrations and prospectus supplements;
- resale registration statements;
- PIPE shares and warrants;
- convertible debt conversion shares;
- stock-based compensation, option pool, RSUs, and warrants;
- earnout shares and SPAC sponsor/promote shares;
- authorized shares versus outstanding shares.

Estimate:

```text
potential dilution = registered/sellable/convertible shares or dollar capacity / current market cap or current shares outstanding
```

If an ATM or shelf is large relative to market cap or float, treat rallies as possible issuance windows until filings prove otherwise.

## 3. Analyze Float and Selling Overhang

Map:

- free float versus basic shares outstanding;
- insider, sponsor, VC, strategic, and employee holdings;
- lockup expiration dates;
- resale eligibility and registration effectiveness;
- historical insider/sponsor/VC selling;
- short interest, borrow availability, borrow cost when free;
- index inclusion/rebalance effects;
- liquidity, average dollar volume, and days-to-sell for large holders.

Ask whether supply of sellable shares is likely to overwhelm demand even if the business improves.

## 4. Stress Debt and Interest Burden

For each debt instrument:

- principal amount;
- cash interest rate and PIK interest;
- amortization;
- maturity wall;
- covenants;
- collateral/security;
- refinancing terms;
- make-whole or prepayment penalties;
- convertibility and dilution terms;
- asset-backed or project-backed recourse.

Stress:

```text
cash interest / revenue
cash interest / gross profit
cash interest / adjusted EBITDA
cash interest / operating cash flow
capex + cash interest versus cash on hand and expected funding
```

High-interest debt can make revenue growth non-accretive to common equity if interest, amortization, or refinancing risk absorbs future FCF.

## 5. Classify Financing Quality

Use qualitative buckets:

- `clean`: cash/debt/float structure does not materially cap common-equity upside.
- `manageable`: dilution or debt exists but is sized reasonably versus growth and cash flow.
- `watch`: overhang is material but may clear with execution, refinancing, lockup expiration, or ATM completion.
- `structural_overhang`: financing terms likely pressure every rally or delay equity appreciation until overhang clears.
- `toxic`: dilution/debt/convertible/warrant structure can repeatedly transfer upside away from common equity.

Every bucket requires evidence and caveats. Do not call financing toxic just because dilution exists; assess size, terms, usage, and whether capital creates value.

## 6. Separate Growth Funding From Equity Value

Ask:

- Does the financing fund assets with durable returns above cost of capital?
- Does debt interest or preferred return eat future FCF?
- Are shareholders funding growth through repeated dilution before unit economics are proven?
- Is the company selling equity into every rally?
- Is the convert capped/hedged in a way that reduces dilution, or open-ended?
- Are strategic investors or customers validating demand, or receiving economics senior to common equity?
- Does the capital stack allow common equity to benefit after the buildout?

## 7. Identify Overhang Clearing Conditions

Proof gates:

- ATM usage slows, completes, or is terminated;
- shelf capacity is reduced or no longer needed;
- lockup expires without heavy selling;
- resale shares are absorbed by market volume;
- debt is refinanced at lower cost;
- convertibles are capped, settled, repurchased, or converted cleanly;
- FCF covers cash interest, maintenance capex, and growth capex;
- asset returns exceed financing cost;
- management commits to dilution discipline and filings confirm it.

Invalidation signals:

- repeated ATM/shelf use into rallies;
- new financing required before unit economics validate;
- cash interest rises faster than gross profit;
- covenant pressure or refinancing at punitive rates;
- widening share count without proportional value creation;
- insiders/sponsors/VCs sell heavily as retail demand improves;
- common equity becomes residual behind creditors, preferreds, or strategic counterparties.

## 8. Output Requirements

For relevant equity research, include:

- financing quality bucket;
- capital-stack table;
- dilution capacity and estimated share/market-cap impact;
- debt/interest burden and maturity wall;
- float, lockup, resale, and selling-overhang map;
- whether business upside accrues to common equity;
- proof gates for overhang clearing;
- what would make the stock attractive after dilution/overhang clears.
